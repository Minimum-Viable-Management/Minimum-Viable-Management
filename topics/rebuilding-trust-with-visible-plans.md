Trust rebounds when leaders write down the recovery plan, share the names of the people who shaped it, and let those most affected help course-correct in public.

## ğŸ”‘ Key Points

- Write the plan where everyone can read it, whether that is a e.g. Google Doc, Slack thread, or Notion page.
  People assume you do not have a strategy if they cannot read it, and they will fill the silence with anxiety.

- Credit the voices you consulted.
  Naming contributors makes it obvious you did not design the fix alone and gives others a clear path to influence the next revision.

- Pair words with action without skipping the explanation.
  Announce what will change, then show the first win quickly so teams get both early clarity and proof you can execute.

- Re-reel the plan every week.
  Ask whether any bullet still applies, update it openly, and explain why you changed course so people see adaptability as listening rather than inconsistency.

- Pre-brief the people whose work is being changed and recruit them into the fix.
  Walk them through your reasoning, offer them a visible role in the transition, and show how their strengths fuel the future rather than erasing their past effort.
